# 🎯 Super Simple: How to Start Sage Reports

You don't need to know anything about coding! Just follow these simple steps:

---

## ✅ The Easiest Way (Right Now!)

I've created a file you can **double-click** to start everything!

### Step 1: Find the File
- Go to your project folder: `Sage Reports`
- Look for a file called: **"Launch Sage Reports.command"**
- It has a little terminal/script icon

### Step 2: Double-Click It!
- Just double-click the file
- A window will open (that's normal - it's starting the app)
- Your browser will open automatically
- **That's it!** The app is running!

### Step 3: Use It!
- The app opens in your browser at `http://localhost:3000`
- You can bookmark this page for easy access later
- Just click the bookmark if the app is already running

---

## 🎨 Make It Even Easier (Optional)

### Add to Your Desktop:

1. **Right-click** the "Launch Sage Reports.command" file
2. Click **"Make Alias"**
3. **Drag the alias** to your Desktop
4. Now you can double-click it from your Desktop!

### Add to Your Dock:

1. **Drag** the "Launch Sage Reports.command" file
2. **Drop it** on your Dock (the bar at the bottom of your screen)
3. Now you can **click it from your Dock** anytime!

---

## 📱 Bookmark the App (For Quick Access)

Once the app is running:

1. **Bookmark** `http://localhost:3000` in your browser
2. **Name it:** "Sage Reports"
3. **Now:** If the app is running, just click the bookmark!

**Note:** The app needs to be started first (using the double-click file), then you can use the bookmark.

---

**Easy way:**

## ❓ Troubleshooting

**"I can't find the file"**
- It's in your project folder: `Sage Reports`
- Look for "Launch Sage Reports.command"

**"It says I can't open it"**
- Right-click the file → "Open With" → "Terminal"
- Or: Right-click → "Get Info" → Check "Open with Terminal"

**"Nothing happens when I double-click"**
- Make sure you're double-clicking "Launch Sage Reports.command"
- Try right-clicking → "Open With" → "Terminal"

**"The browser doesn't open"**
- Wait a few seconds (it takes time to start)
- Or manually go to: `http://localhost:3000`
